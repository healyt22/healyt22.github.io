Deriving Political Party Affiliation with Diffusion Maps

Paper

Natural language processing has a large computational burden due to the high dimensionality and sparsity of textual data. Diffusion Maps is a mathematical technique that can be used to reduce the dimensionality of data, even if the data has been sampled from a nonliner underlying structure. This paper will employ and evaluate Diffusion maps as a dimensionality reduction technique for textual data, using it to derive the political party for members of the United States Congress based on Twitter activity.

Bootstrapping Handwritten Digits

Paper

Multilayer Neural Networks often require a lot of training data, which can be intractable to collect. This project evaluates whether resampled data can be an effective means of increasing the predictive accuracy of a neural network. Using the MNIST handwritten digits dataset, resampling methods such as bootstrapping, rejection sampling, and randomization methods will be employed to generate additional data that will be used to train the network. Performance will then be evaluated against the network trained with only the original set. The resampling methods will be implemented with R, and the neural network will be implemented in Python.
